Air Pollution

The visualization for comparison of GHG emissions and population is created in Tableau. The left graph is the comparison of GHG emissions to population in 2006. The blue rectangular bars represent GHG emissions in million tonnes while the pink rectangular bars represent the population in millions. Two rectangular bars are stacked for each country. The graph shows that there is no positive relationship between population and GHG emissions. For example, Mexico has larger amount of population but smaller emissions. In most countries, larger population indicates larger amount of emissions. Other five years show similar pattern.
